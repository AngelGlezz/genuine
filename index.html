One Goal

Soccer has been defined as the “international language,” having the ability to bring people together over sport, health, and a sense of belonging.
However, for people with intellectual or neurological differences, soccer is often a language they are excluded from speaking.

With One Goal we want to change this. By going beyond spectatorship to active engagement, athletes with intellectual and neurological differences become stronger, healthier, and more self-assured, thus allowing them to engage more confidently in their broader communities.
